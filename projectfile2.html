5th Street Field Recording (Draft)

What it was supposed to be

A sound piece built from a straight walk down 5th Street. Hill Street to San Pedro Street. No cuts. No smoothing. Just the real mix of traffic, people, footsteps, storefronts, idling buses, and all the small moments you only notice when you listen instead of look.

What exists instead

A handful of voice memos recorded on my phone, now residing on an unlabeled thumbdrive. Some are blown out. Some are too quiet. There is one clip where the mic caught a man yelling jokes at passing cars and another where someone is dragging a shopping cart for an entire block. The folder is messy but the textures are honest.

Why it stalled

  • I kept trying to make the street sound cleaner than it is.
  • The recordings felt too heavy to treat as simple audio material.
  • Every attempt to mix it turned into a different project.

What this fragment reveals

That the city resists being reduced to a neat narrative. That field recording is also a form of witnessing. That maybe the draft is closer to the truth than any polished version would have been.
