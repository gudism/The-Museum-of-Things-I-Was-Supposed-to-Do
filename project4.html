LA Contracts Bot (Prototype)

What it was supposed to be

A small bot that could scan public contract data from Los Angeles and flag anything that looked unusual. Not a full investigation, just a background process that noticed patterns the city preferred to ignore.

What exists instead

A folder called:

data_raw

that never filled the way it was supposed to.

python run_anomaly_detector.py \ --input data_raw/contracts_sample.csv \ --output reports/anomalies.csv

The command looks convincing. The data does not exist yet.

Why it stalled

  • The city transparency infrastructure is built to slow you down.
  • Public records requests took more energy than the actual code.
  • The bureaucracy had more stamina than I did.
  • The story kept expanding and the data never arrived on time.

What this fragment reveals

That corruption in Los Angeles is a structural habit, not a glitch. That a bot meant to find anomalies needs data the city refuses to give. That sometimes the failure is the evidence.
