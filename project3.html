Audio Reactive Visuals (No Audio)

What it was supposed to be

A growing collection of audio reactive TouchDesigner visuals that would eventually respond to original sound. I wanted each piece to move in a way that felt like it was listening. Shapes that tremble with bass. Lines that shiver with higher frequencies. A small universe of motion tied to something you could hear.

What exists instead

Visuals that are in motion but not actually reacting to anything outside themselves. They pulse and distort as if music is present, but the input is internal. They are reacting to nothing and still moving anyway.

Why it stalled

The sound never caught up to the visuals. Each network was waiting for a track that did not exist. The patches moved on their own and stayed that way.

What this fragment reveals

That motion can be a habit. That a system can pretend to be reactive even when it is alone. That the gap between sound and image is its own kind of space.
